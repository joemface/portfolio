About Me

I am a former music major transformed into a computer scientist because I can see the immense creativity there is within technology. Growing up, all I knew to do was type my thoughts in notepad and play pinball while trying to guess how to play mine sweeper. I knew of computer programming but always figured it was out of my realm of capabilities. Jump to 2014 when my aspirations switched from making music to gaining knowledge in college with computer programming, knowing the task at hand would be challenging and unforgiving, but it would be well worth the time in the end.

As my Bachelors degree comes to an end, I was granted the opportunity to be part of a team of six developers to create a lunar landing simulator to observe particles and their motion for safety. The project is called PELT 2.0 and we aim to create the most accurately realistic moon simulation. My contributions can be found in my resumé.
